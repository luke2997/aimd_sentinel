5. Vendor due-diligence questions

  1. What exact clinical workflow and user role was the device validated for, and does that match our proposed deployment?
  2. What patient populations, scanner/protocol types, and site types were included in validation?
  3. What is the required human oversight step before clinical action is taken?
  4. How are software/model updates validated, communicated, and rolled back if problems occur?
  5. What local performance-monitoring dashboard or export does the vendor provide after deployment?
  6. Which software versions were affected by the reported calculation/classification anomalies, and what CAPA or field action closed them?
  7. What validation set and acceptance criteria are used for clinical measurements/classifications after each software release?
  8. How should users verify or override questionable calculated outputs before they affect clinical or procedural decisions?

6. Suggested local monitoring plan

  1. Monthly case volume processed by the AI device.
  2. Number and proportion of failed/errored cases.
  3. Clinician override, disagreement, or feedback events.
  4. Representative false-positive and false-negative review sample.
  5. Turnaround-time or notification-latency before and after deployment.
  6. Scanner/protocol/site distribution of processed cases, where applicable.
  7. All software/model version changes with pre/post-change checks.
  8. Track calculated-output disagreements by software version, measurement type, user, and case type.
  9. Review a sample of software-generated measurements/classifications against expert manual review after each release.
  10. Maintain a local log of vendor CAPA/field-action notices and confirm installed versions are not affected.
